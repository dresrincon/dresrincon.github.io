Joschmi was started by Joost Schmidt—a typographer, designer, and overlooked pioneer of Germany’s Bauhaus movement—and finished by Flavia Zimbardi—a Brazilian designer (Velimirovic: “Hidden”). Zimbardi took an unfinished draft of a stencil alphabet that Schmidt made in 1930 and created a full character set complete with capital letters. The letterforms consist of circle segments, square strokes, rounded terminals and well-placed white space, that provide character, legibility, and the distinctively geometric Bauhaus look (“Hidden”)
