Reross revives the alphabet designs of Reinhold Rossig—a student of Bauhaus typographer Joost Schmidt (“Hidden”). Nearly ninety years after Rossig drafted an alphabet, Elia Preuss, a German typography student, created a typeface out of it by complementing Rossig’s letterforms with the poster designs of Rossig’s peer Herman Werner Kubsch ("Devroye";“Partnership”). The letterforms explore width and x-height, and resemble Schmidt’s work in their monolinear, geometric, sans-serif appearance, and construction using a compass and ruler (Faller).
